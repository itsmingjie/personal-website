My name is Megan Cui.

I'm a senior at William Mason High School in suburban Cincinnati, OH and an incoming freshman at Harvard University. I plan on pursuing economics, computer science, and philosophy while I'm there. As a career, I'd like to go into developing legal technology or IP/patent law.

I've done a lot of work when it comes to technology exposure and leadership in my community because I encountered tech by chance through attending a local hackathon my sophomore year. So, I give back by running hackathons with some of my best friends from Hack Club and Mason Hack Club.

My experience as a young girl interested tech from the Midwest has given me unique insight on barriers to entry in the tech industry, and, along with media coverage, I've been invited to speak about it at NetJets, TEDxMasonHighSchool, TechOlympics, and comSpark in front of audiences of students, educators, and corporate executives alike.

At heart, I'm also a social activist, leading change in mental health awareness and cultural diversity. I've even spoken about it.

In my free time, I like petting my cat, Geno, blogging, browsing r/aww, and debating.
